## 1. Brand Personality

Finance speaks like a **knowledgeable friend who respects your autonomy**. Not a financial advisor who lectures. Not a gamified app that patronizes. Not a faceless corporation that harvests your data.

### If Finance Were a Person

- **Age/energy:** Late 20s–early 30s — mature enough to be competent, young enough to be approachable
- **Relationship to user:** A trusted friend who's good with money — explains things clearly, never judges, always available but never pushy
- **Communication style:** Direct, warm, concise — says what needs to be said without filler
- **Values:** Privacy, autonomy, clarity, inclusion, honesty

### Brand Archetype: The Sage + The Companion

- **Sage:** Knowledgeable, clear, educational — helps users understand their finances
- **Companion:** Supportive, present, non-judgmental — walks alongside users on their financial journey

## 2. Voice Attributes

Every piece of Finance communication — from app store listings to in-app copy to social media — should embody these five attributes:

### 2.1 Clear

We use plain language. Financial concepts are explained, not assumed. Every sentence has a purpose.

- ✅ "Track where your money goes in under 30 seconds"
- ❌ "Leverage our comprehensive financial management solution to optimize your pecuniary workflows"

### 2.2 Empowering

We help users feel capable and in control. We present facts and options, then let users decide.

- ✅ "You've used 110% of your Food budget — want to adjust?"
- ❌ "You overspent on Food! You need to cut back!"

### 2.3 Honest

We tell the truth about what Finance is and isn't. We don't oversell, exaggerate, or make claims we can't back up. We're upfront about limitations.

- ✅ "Finance is in active development — some features are coming soon"
- ❌ "The most revolutionary financial app ever created"

### 2.4 Warm

We're human and approachable, not corporate or robotic. We use natural language without being overly casual.

- ✅ "Welcome back! Pick up where you left off."
- ❌ "Session resumed. Previous state restored."

### 2.5 Respectful

We never assume financial status, literacy level, or ability. We never guilt, shame, or pressure. We respect user time, attention, and autonomy.

- ✅ "Add a transaction when you're ready — there's no rush"
- ❌ "You haven't logged a transaction today! Don't break your streak!"

## 3. Tone Spectrum

The voice stays consistent; the tone adapts to context. Think of voice as personality and tone as mood.

| Context                     | Tone                                    | Example                                                                                            |
| --------------------------- | --------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Onboarding**              | Warm, encouraging, patient              | "Let's get you set up. This takes about 2 minutes — or skip ahead and explore on your own."        |
| **Daily use**               | Efficient, quiet, informative           | "$47 spent today. On track this week."                                                             |
| **Achievement**             | Celebratory, genuine (not over-the-top) | "Emergency fund fully funded! That took dedication."                                               |
| **Over budget**             | Neutral, factual, supportive            | "You've used 110% of your Dining budget. Want to adjust or roll with it?"                          |
| **Error state**             | Calm, helpful, solution-oriented        | "Something went wrong saving that transaction. Your data is safe — try again?"                     |
| **Empty state**             | Inviting, actionable, low-pressure      | "No transactions yet. Add your first one — it takes 3 taps."                                       |
| **Privacy messaging**       | Confident, factual, empowering          | "Your financial data is encrypted on your device. We can't read it — and we designed it that way." |
| **Marketing / external**    | Confident, differentiated, accessible   | "The financial tracker that works offline, stays private, and never judges your spending."         |
| **Upgrade prompt**          | Informational, honest, no-pressure      | "Premium includes multi-device sync and AI insights. Here's what's included."                      |
| **Returning after absence** | Welcoming, no-guilt, warm               | "Welcome back! Your data is right where you left it."                                              |

## 4. Vocabulary Guide

### Preferred Terms

| Instead of...                 | Use...                             | Why                                    |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| User                          | Person, you                        | People aren't "users" — they're people |
| Overspent                     | Used more than planned             | Factual, not judgmental                |
| Budget violation              | Over your plan                     | Non-punitive language                  |
| Failed / failure              | Didn't meet the target             | Growth-oriented framing                |
| Sync your data                | Keep your devices in sync          | People-centered, not data-centered     |
| Data collection               | What we store (and where)          | Transparent and specific               |
| Free tier / freemium          | Free forever (core features)       | Honest about the long-term commitment  |
| Premium upsell                | Premium features                   | No sales pressure implied              |
| Notifications / alerts        | Updates, snapshots, check-ins      | Less intrusive connotation             |
| Bank-grade security           | Encrypted on your device           | Specific and verifiable                |
| Revolutionary / game-changing | Different, thoughtful, intentional | Honest, not hyperbolic                 |
| Simple / easy                 | Clear, quick, straightforward      | Accessible without being condescending |

### Financial Terms — Always Explain

When using financial terminology, always pair it with a plain-language explanation on first use:

| Term                  | Plain Explanation                                                  |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Envelope budgeting    | Give every dollar a purpose — when a category is spent, it's spent |
| Zero-based budget     | A plan where every dollar of income has a destination              |
| Net worth             | What you own minus what you owe                                    |
| Savings rate          | The portion of your income you're keeping                          |
| Recurring transaction | Something you pay regularly — like rent or a subscription          |
| Amortization          | How a loan balance decreases over time with each payment           |
| Compound interest     | Interest that earns interest — it grows faster over time           |

### Words We Never Use

| Word/Phrase              | Why                                                  |
| ------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| "You need to..."         | Prescriptive — we inform, not instruct               |
| "Don't miss out"         | Artificial urgency — FOMO is a dark pattern          |
| "Limited time"           | Manufactured scarcity                                |
| "Exclusive" (for access) | Creates artificial hierarchy                         |
| "Just" (minimizing)      | "Just enter your data" minimizes effort              |
| "Obviously" / "Simply"   | Assumes knowledge, alienates beginners               |
| "Guilty pleasure"        | Judgment-laden                                       |
| "Splurge" / "Waste"      | Value judgments on spending choices                  |
| "Financial freedom"      | Overused, means different things to different people |
| "Crush your goals"       | Aggressive, not our personality                      |
| "Hustle"                 | Implies overwork as a virtue                         |

## 5. Do / Don't Examples

### App Store Descriptions

| ✅ Do                                                 | ❌ Don't                                                 |
| ----------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| "Track your spending in under 30 seconds"             | "The ultimate all-in-one financial management solution"  |
| "Your financial data stays on your device, encrypted" | "Bank-grade military-level security protects your data"  |
| "Works offline — no internet required"                | "Revolutionary offline-first architecture"               |
| "Adapts to your comfort level with money"             | "Even financial novices can use our intuitive interface" |
| "Free forever — no hidden limits on core features"    | "Unlock your financial potential with our premium tier"  |

### In-App Copy

| ✅ Do                                                  | ❌ Don't                                               |
| ------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| "You've used 110% of your Food plan — want to adjust?" | "⚠️ OVERSPENT! You exceeded your Food budget by $23!"  |
| "3-week streak of tracking daily!"                     | "Don't break your streak! Log a transaction now!"      |
| "No transactions yet. Add one — it takes 3 taps."      | "Your transaction list is empty. Get started NOW!"     |
| "Welcome back! Your data is right where you left it."  | "You've been away for 12 days. You missed logging $X." |
| "Premium includes multi-device sync and AI insights."  | "Upgrade NOW to unlock the FULL Finance experience!"   |

### Social Media

| ✅ Do                                                                          | ❌ Don't                                                    |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| "We built Finance because we wanted a budget app that doesn't sell your data." | "TIRED of apps that STEAL your financial data?? 😱"         |
| "Your spending data shouldn't live on someone else's server."                  | "Big Tech is watching every dollar you spend!!"             |
| "Finance works offline. Your money, your device, your rules."                  | "Unlike OTHER apps, we actually care about your privacy 🙄" |
| "We designed Finance for people who feel overwhelmed by money apps."           | "If you can't handle spreadsheets, try Finance!"            |

### Press / Media

| ✅ Do                                                                                                  | ❌ Don't                                                             |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "Finance is a multi-platform financial tracker with an offline-first, privacy-by-design architecture." | "Finance is disrupting the $X billion personal finance market."      |
| "We chose to encrypt data on-device because financial data is deeply personal."                        | "We're the only app that truly protects your privacy."               |
| "Our expertise-tiered UI adapts terminology and complexity to each person's comfort level."            | "Even the most financially illiterate users can understand our app." |

## 7. Elevator Pitches

### 15-Second Pitch

> "Finance is a free, multi-platform budget tracker that works offline and keeps your data encrypted on your device. No bank connection needed, no data harvesting — just clear financial tracking in under 30 seconds a day."

### 30-Second Pitch

> "Finance is a personal budget tracker built for people who want financial clarity without giving up their privacy. It works offline on iOS, Android, Web, and Windows, encrypts all your data on your device, and adapts to your comfort level with money — whether you're a spreadsheet person or you've never budgeted before. The core app is free forever. No bank connection required. No data selling. Just honest financial tracking."

### 60-Second Pitch

> "Most financial apps ask for your bank password, upload your data to their servers, and show you anxiety-inducing alerts about overspending. We built Finance because we thought there should be a better way.
>
> Finance is a multi-platform financial tracker — native on iOS, Android, Web, and Windows — that keeps all your data encrypted on your device. It works fully offline, syncs between your devices only if you choose, and never requires a bank connection. The app adapts to your comfort level: plain language for beginners, full detail for power users, and cognitive accessibility features for people who find existing finance apps overwhelming.
>
> We observe and inform — we never judge. Over budget? We'll let you know and ask if you'd like to adjust. No red warnings, no guilt trips.
>
> The core financial tracker is free forever. Premium adds AI insights and multi-device sync. We make money from subscriptions, not your data. Finance is source-available under the Business Source License — you can read every line of code that handles your financial information."

## 9. Channel-Specific Adaptations

### App Store Listings

- **Tone:** Confident, benefit-focused, accessible
- **Style:** Short sentences. Bullet points. Feature → benefit structure.
- **Avoid:** Superlatives, competitor bashing, technical jargon
- **Constraint:** Character limits are sacred — every character earns its place

### Social Media (Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Threads)

- **Tone:** Conversational, authentic, occasionally opinionated (about privacy, never about people)
- **Style:** First person plural ("We built..." / "We believe..."). Thread-friendly for longer stories.
- **Hashtags:** #PrivacyFirst #OfflineFirst #Budgeting #PersonalFinance #OpenSource
- **Avoid:** Engagement bait, artificial urgency, dunking on competitors by name

### Blog / Long-Form Content

- **Tone:** Thoughtful, educational, story-driven
- **Style:** Show the "why" behind decisions. Use concrete examples. Link to architecture docs for technical depth.
- **Audience:** Split between technical (KMP, privacy architecture) and general (budgeting tips, financial clarity)
- **Avoid:** Thought-leader jargon, "we're disrupting..." language

### Press / Media

- **Tone:** Professional, factual, differentiated
- **Style:** AP style. Lead with the most newsworthy angle. Include specific numbers and verifiable claims.
- **Always include:** Platform count, privacy architecture summary, free-forever positioning, BSL license
- **Avoid:** Superlatives, unverifiable claims, founder mythology

### Community (Reddit, Hacker News, GitHub Discussions)

- **Tone:** Peer-to-peer, humble, helpful
- **Style:** Answer questions directly. Share what you learned. Be honest about limitations.
- **Avoid:** Marketing-speak, self-promotion disguised as advice, astroturfing

### In-App Copy

- **Tone:** Quiet, efficient, supportive
- **Style:** Minimum words for maximum clarity. Contextual — appears when needed, disappears when not.
- **Avoid:** Exclamation points (except genuine celebrations), ALL CAPS, emoji overuse

## 10. Review Checklist

Before publishing any Finance marketing content, verify:

- [ ] **Non-judgmental:** Does any sentence shame, guilt, or pressure the reader?
- [ ] **Privacy-accurate:** Are all privacy/security claims verifiable against architecture docs (ADR-0003, ADR-0004)?
- [ ] **Inclusive:** Does the content assume income level, financial literacy, ability, or technical knowledge?
- [ ] **Honest:** Are there any superlatives, exaggerations, or claims about unshipped features?
- [ ] **Accessible:** Is the language plain enough for a general audience? (Target: Flesch-Kincaid ≤ 8th grade)
- [ ] **Autonomy-respecting:** Does any call-to-action use urgency, scarcity, or social pressure?
- [ ] **Brand-consistent:** Does the tone match the context (see Section 3)?
- [ ] **Technically grounded:** Do feature claims match the current state of the codebase?
- [ ] **Competitor-respectful:** Do we focus on what Finance does differently, not what others do wrong?
- [ ] **Character limits:** For store listings, are all character limits verified?
